Being a Web Developer wasn't my plan, but... here I am CRUSHING it.

Web Development snuck up on me when I was least expecting it. Layout design has been part of my life for over 15 years, and I recently found msyelf taking a chance-step into the realm of coding and programming.

I'm not your average code-er or developer. My long history of DIY coding, blazing though WYISWYG site builders, and an extensive background of photography, magazine/layout design, and digital content makes me different than the rest... I had everything I needed, I just needed to learn the syntax of a for loop.

Every day I become a better programmer. Stack that on top of already extraordinary visual design and concept skills, it becomes truly a force to be reckoned with.

Equal parts creative and developer, I find that each side makes me better at the other. Knowing how to develop affects my design in a way that is efficient to produce, has desirable UX qualities and my creative brain, and attention to branding and UI immediately send my projects soaring beyond average developers.

Being an Ever-Learner I've surpassed mere visual web design with my understanding of complex development and programming method, MVC and Object Oriented Programming. I am fiercly committed to producing fast, mobile responsive designs that prioritize WAVE accessibility compliance as I work with a team to develop applications that are impactful and matter.

The truth is that I don't know everything, and I definitely don't look the part, but I'm really good at learning the things. This has given me a unique ability to translate programmer "Speak" in order to communicate with non-developer stake holders and clients.
